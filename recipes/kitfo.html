KITFO (Ethiopian Traditional Food)

Kitfo, Ethiopian Cuisine consists of minced raw beef, marinated in mitmita (a chili powder-based spice blend) and niter kibbeh (a clarified butter infused with herbs and spices).

Kitfo cooked lightly rare is known as kitfo leb leb. Kitfo is often served alongside-sometimes mixed with- a mild cheese called ayibe or cooked greens known as gomen. In many parts of Ethiopia, kitfo is served with injera, a flatbread made from teff, although in traditional Gurage cuisine, one would use kocho, a thick flatbread made from the ensete plant. An ensete leaf may be used as a garnish. Though not considered a delicacy, kitfo is generally held in high regard.

Ingridents

  • 2 lbs top round beef (freshly cut)
  • 6 teaspoons ground cayenne pepper (Mitmita*)
  • 4 tablespoons clarified butter (Nitir Kebe)
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder (korerima)
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • salt and black pepper

Here is how to prepare kitfo

  1. Cut the meat in small pieces; remove fat and seams.
  2. In a food processor put small amount of the meat at a time, spread some mitmita, grind until meat is finely chopped; using a fork, remove any residue of fat and seams; place the spicy ground meat in a dish; repeat the process until all required meat is done.
  3. In small pot, on a lower heat melt the butter, add the remaining mitmita, false cardamom, (garlic, black pepper, salt, if needed); remove from heat.
  4. Combine the spicy ground meat with the spicy butter; using fork and spoon, mix it until completely marinated.
  5. Place it in a lightly warm serving dish, serve it immediately with Injera or bread. If preferred, saut√© the marinated meat for two to three minutes.
